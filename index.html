    Upstate New York is a geographic region consisting of the portion of New York State lying north of the New York City metropolitan area.[1] Although the precise boundary is debated, Upstate New York excludes New York City and Long Island, and most definitions of the region exclude all or part of Westchester and Rockland counties.
